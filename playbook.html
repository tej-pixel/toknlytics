Penny’s Playbook

19 plays. Six families. Every plan gets all of them.

Each play pairs a detector that watches your live traffic with an action Penny can run. When the pattern shows up in your data, the play appears on your dashboard with a savings estimate computed on your own usage — free to see, and Penny runs it when you add her. New plays ship monthly.

Routing

Family 01 · Usually the biggest, fastest win

Send every call to the cheapest model, provider and path that clears the quality bar.

Smart Router Flagship

Per-call routing to the cheapest capable model; Penny learns quality thresholds from real outcomes.

Penny detects: Simple tasks (classification, extraction, short summaries) running on frontier models.
Typical impact: 30–70% on routed traffic

Provider Arbitrage

Same model, cheapest endpoint — direct vs Azure vs Bedrock, including regional pricing.

Penny detects: An identical model available cheaper on another provider you already use.
Typical impact: 5–15%

Escalation Ladder

Try cheap first; auto-escalate to the big model only on low confidence or failed output.

Penny detects: One model choice across wildly varying task difficulty.
Typical impact: 20–40%

Prompt & Context

Family 02 · The invisible tax

Most input spend is tokens nobody needed to send. These plays shrink it without touching output quality.

Prompt Trim

Strips redundant instructions, stale few-shot examples and dead branches; ships a tested slimmer prompt.

Penny detects: Input-to-output token ratio far above workload norm; large static prefix on every call.
Typical impact: 10–30% of input

Context Compressor

Sliding-window + summarization of conversation history instead of resending everything.

Penny detects: Tokens per session growing linearly with turn count.
Typical impact: 20–50% on chat

RAG Right-Sizer

Tunes chunk size and count, adds reranking so fewer chunks are sent per query.

Penny detects: Large retrieval payloads where answers cite only 1–2 of many chunks.
Typical impact: 15–40% on RAG

Caching

Family 03 · Highest-margin family

Most teams have provider caching off, or structured so it never hits. Near-zero cost to fix.

Prompt Cache Enabler Flagship

Turns on and correctly structures provider-native prompt caching (cached reads can be ~90% cheaper).

Penny detects: Repeated identical prompt prefixes with no cache-hit billing lines.
Typical impact: Up to 80% of cacheable input

Semantic Cache

Serves semantically-duplicate requests from cache instead of re-calling the model.

Penny detects: Clusters of near-identical requests — support bots, FAQ-shaped traffic.
Typical impact: 10–35%

Waste Control

Family 04 · Easiest savings to verify

This family eliminates pure loss — spend that produced nothing. The before/after is unambiguous.

Token Governor

Enforces max-token caps, stop sequences and structured-output schemas per endpoint.

Penny detects: Output overruns; verbose responses where downstream parses a single field.
Typical impact: 5–20% of output

Retry Sweeper

Kills duplicate-spend patterns: timeout retries that double-bill, 429 storms, failed calls that still burned tokens.

Penny detects: Error-coded spend; burst retry signatures.
Typical impact: 3–10% of total

Loop Breaker

Agent-specific: step caps, repeated-tool-call detection, runaway-chain circuit breakers.

Penny detects: Long agent traces; identical tool calls repeating within one run.
Typical impact: Variable — often dramatic

Scheduling & Structural

Family 05 · Bigger commitment, bigger payoff

Changes to how work is shaped, not just how calls are made.

Batch Shifter

Moves latency-tolerant jobs to provider Batch APIs at their flat ~50% discount.

Penny detects: Cron-shaped traffic; embeddings and enrichment jobs on realtime endpoints.
Typical impact: 50% on shifted traffic

Distillery Project

Fine-tunes / distills a small model to replace a frontier model on one high-volume, narrow task.

Penny detects: Very high volume of homogeneous requests on an expensive model.
Typical impact: 70–90% on that task

Embedding Optimizer

Dedupes re-embedded content, downshifts embedding models where recall permits, caches results.

Penny detects: Repeat embeddings of unchanged documents.
Typical impact: 30–60% of embedding

Governance, Margin & Green

Family 06 · Product-line plays

Plays tied to Ledger, Margin and the Green Score — prevention, allocation and carbon-aware savings.

Budget Sentinel Ledger

Per-team and per-key budgets, kill switches, anomaly alerts.

Penny detects: No spend limits on live API keys; historic anomaly spikes.
Typical impact: Incident prevention

Chargeback Engine Ledger

Allocates spend to teams and products with showback reports — makes Ledger the finance source of truth.

Penny detects: Multi-team spend with no cost allocation.
Typical impact: Finance source of truth

Margin Guard Flagship

Detects margin-negative customers and auto-remediates: throttle, cheaper tier for that customer's traffic, or repricing alert.

Penny detects: Per-customer COGS exceeding revenue.
Typical impact: Recovered margin

Plan Aligner Margin

Maps per-customer cost curves onto pricing tiers and flags where the pricing model leaks.

Penny detects: Cost-per-customer variance far wider than plan-price variance.
Typical impact: Pricing intelligence

Green Router

Carbon-aware routing to lower-carbon regions and models when quality-equivalent. Cheaper and greener are usually the same route.

Penny detects: High-carbon model/region mix flagged by your Green Score.
Typical impact: CO₂e ↓ + 5–20% cost
