How TokenIQ compares

There are great tools for routing AI traffic and for debugging it. TokenIQ answers a different question: what does it cost, what's its footprint, and am I margin-positive? Here's where each fits.

The three categories near us

  • AI gateways (e.g. LiteLLM, Cloudflare AI Gateway) — sit in the request path to route, retry and cache across providers. Great for control; cost is a side view.
  • LLM observability (e.g. Helicone, Langfuse) — trace prompts, latency, quality and evals. Built for debugging behaviour, not financial reporting.
  • Provider dashboards (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.) — show spend for that one provider, with no cross-provider, per-customer or carbon view.

TokenIQ is the cost, carbon and margin layer that sits across all of them — read-only, every provider, and built for the finance question rather than the engineering one.

When to use what

If you need to route and retry across providers in real time, use a gateway. If you're debugging prompt behaviour and evals, use observability. If you need to know what your AI costs, what it emits, and whether each customer is profitable — and report it to finance — that's TokenIQ. Many teams run a gateway and TokenIQ: one controls the traffic, the other owns the economics.
