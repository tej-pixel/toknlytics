The Green Score

Your AI's footprint, in one grade

Every prompt has a carbon cost. The Green Score turns your real token usage into a single, comparable grade — built from model efficiency and the energy behind it — so you can shrink it as you optimize.

How the Green Score works

It's one number built from three measurable inputs — the same method whether you're an individual or an enterprise:

Tokens usedper model & app × Model efficiencyenergy / token × Grid carbonwhere it runs = Green Scoreone clear grade

We combine published energy-per-token benchmarks for each model with regional grid-carbon intensity for the datacenter it ran in, then translate the result into CO₂e, energy (kWh) and water (for cooling) — and a letter grade from A to F. Because it's consistent and comparable, you can watch it climb the moment you right-size models or trim prompts.

Figures are estimates built from published benchmarks and regional grid data — designed to be consistent and comparable rather than a certified audit. Full methodology and sources are available in-app.

Why it matters

  • It's an ESG line item now. AI energy use is showing up in sustainability reporting — the Green Score gives you an auditable, exportable number.
  • Greener usually means cheaper. The same moves that cut carbon — smaller models, trimmed prompts, caching — cut the bill too.
  • It's comparable. One grade you can track over time and benchmark across teams, apps and models.
