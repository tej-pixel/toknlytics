How to cut your LLM bill

Five moves that reliably reduce AI spend — without hurting quality. Most teams find their first big win in an afternoon.

AI costs creep because no one owns the number. These are the highest-leverage levers, roughly in order of impact — and how to apply them without breaking what works.

1. Measure first

You can't cut what you can't see. Before changing anything, get spend broken down by model, app, feature and (if you resell AI) customer. The breakdown almost always surprises people: a single endpoint or one heavy prompt is usually doing most of the damage. Meter does this free, read-only, in minutes.

2. Right-size the model

The biggest lever by far. Frontier models can cost 20–50× a small one for the same tokens. Route easy, high-volume calls (classification, extraction, short replies) to a smaller model and keep the frontier model for the genuinely hard tasks. Many teams cut 30–50% here alone.

3. Trim your prompts

Oversized system prompts and stuffed context inflate the input side of every call. Cut boilerplate, summarize long context, and stop resending what the model doesn't need. A prompt analyzer makes the bloat obvious.

4. Cache repeat work

Identical and near-identical requests are pure waste. Cache responses (and use provider-native prompt caching where available) to stop paying twice for the same answer.

5. Alert on anomalies

The worst bills come from surprises — a runaway loop, an abusive user, a deploy that 10×'d traffic overnight. Set budgets and anomaly alerts so you hear about it the same day, not on the invoice.

Bonus: it's greener too. Every one of these moves also cuts energy and carbon — so your Green Score climbs as your bill drops.
